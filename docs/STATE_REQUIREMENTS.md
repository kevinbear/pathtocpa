# 🇺🇸 CPA requirements by state — research draft

> ⚠️ **DRAFT for review — verify before building on it.** Compiled from NASBA, state boards,
> AICPA/ThisWayToCPA, and state CPA societies (web research, 2026). CPA rules are **changing
> fast** right now: the 2024–2025 AICPA/NASBA model-law update is adding a "bachelor's + 2
> years' experience" pathway in many states through 2025–2027. **Cells marked _verify_ were
> not confirmed against binding board-rule text.** Always confirm against the official state
> board before relying on any value. This is a planning aid, not legal advice.

Goal: a per-state map of (A) what's required to **sit for the CPA Exam** and (B) what's
required for **licensure**, to inform extending PathToCPA beyond California. See
[Implementation notes](#-implementation-notes-for-multi-state-support) at the bottom.

**Legend:** units = semester hours unless noted · "may sit at 120" = can take the exam before
finishing 150 · 🆕 = new/alt pathway adopted under the 2025–2027 model-law wave.

## 🔑 Patterns worth noting

- **Sitting for the exam:** the large majority let you **sit at 120 units** (bachelor's), with the usual ~24 accounting + ~24 business. Outliers: **South Dakota** (needs 150 to sit), **Connecticut**/**Arizona**/**Ohio** (need the full degree/120 with no partial-sit, or specific structure), **Kansas** (full 150 course profile).
- **Licensure (traditional):** almost universally **150 total units + ~24–30 accounting + ~24 business + 1 year experience**.
- **The 2025–2027 wave 🆕:** dozens of states are adding a **"bachelor's (120) + 2 years' experience"** pathway (no 150). Already live or imminent in **AK, CT, GA, MN, MT, NJ, NM, NC, OH, OR, PA, SC, TN, VA, WA, WV, WI, DC** and more — California's **AB 1175 (Jan 1, 2027)** is the same shape.
- **Ethics requirement varies in 3 flavors:** (1) **AICPA ethics exam/course** (most states), (2) **state-specific** (TX 4-hr course, NC 8-hr law course, OH PSR, WI/NV own exam, NJ law course, Utah Laws & Rules), (3) **none** (FL, GA, KY, NH, MI, MA, NY, PA, WY…).
- **Quirks to model:** Alabama requires **U.S. citizenship**; **ID, KS, MS, OK(?), WY, LA(?)** require **residency**; experience is **2 years** by default in a few even traditionally (IN, ME).

## 🛠 Implementation notes for multi-state support

This research is meant to seed a generalized rules engine. Today the app hardcodes California
in `src/lib/rules/california.ts` (shape: `RuleSet` with `sit` and `license` requirements) and
the engine in `src/lib/eligibility/evaluate.ts` is already **data-driven off that rule object**.
A multi-state design likely needs:

1. **A `RuleSet` per state** (same shape as `california.ts`), keyed by state code — e.g.
   `src/lib/rules/states/{ca,ny,tx,...}.ts` + a registry/index.
2. **New fields** the CA model doesn't yet capture, surfaced by this table:
   - `sitUnits` (120 vs 150) and a `canSitBefore150` flag.
   - `pathways[]` — states now have **multiple licensure pathways** (traditional 150+1yr,
     master's+1yr, bachelor's+2yr). The journey/eligibility logic must compare across pathways
     (we already do a version of this for CA's AB 1175).
   - `ethics` as a small union: `{ type: "aicpa" | "state" | "none", label, url }`.
   - `experienceYears` (1 vs 2) per pathway; `residencyRequired`, `citizenshipRequired` booleans.
   - `effectiveDate` / `sunsetDate` per pathway (the law is time-phased — e.g. CA legacy
     sunsets 2028-12-31). The engine should pick valid pathways for "today."
3. **A state picker** in the UI (currently the app says "California only" in many places —
   Footer, FAQ, About). Those copy strings + the `diagnose`/journey/cost templates would need
   to become state-aware.
4. **Per-state cost templates** (fees differ) — lower priority.

Suggested first step: implement **2–3 high-value states** (e.g. NY, TX, plus CA) behind a state
selector to prove the abstraction, then expand. Keep the "verify" cells in this doc resolved
against official board text **before** any state ships.
